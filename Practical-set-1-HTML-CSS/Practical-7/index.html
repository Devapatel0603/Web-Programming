Ahmedabad

India's first Heritage city

History

  • Ahmedabad, founded in 1411 AD by Sultan Ahmad Shah I, served as the capital of the Sultanate of Gujarat, thriving as a hub of trade and commerce.
  • Under Mughal rule in the 16th century, Ahmedabad witnessed architectural developments, including mosques and palaces.
  • The city played a pivotal role in India's struggle for independence, with Mahatma Gandhi establishing the Sabarmati Ashram here in 1917.
  • Post-independence, Ahmedabad became Gujarat's commercial capital, experiencing rapid urbanization and industrial growth.
  • It is renowned for its textile industry and diverse economy, spanning sectors such as pharmaceuticals and
    • information technology.
  • Ahmedabad's cultural heritage is rich, with a blend of Hindu, Islamic, and colonial-era architecture earning
    • it
  • UNESCO World Heritage City status in 2017.
  • The city hosts vibrant festivals and is celebrated for its cuisine and handicrafts, reflecting its cultural
    • vibrancy.
  • Ahmedabad continues to evolve as a dynamic economic and cultural center, embodying resilience and adaptability.
